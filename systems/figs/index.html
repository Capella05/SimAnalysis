Modelling challenge

Here are provisional reference results, which we can compare with SpaghettiLens models.

All sizes are in pixels.

On the left we have arrival-time contours for the peak point of the source. The minima and saddle points here are the images of that point source. The white regions around those point images approximate the images due a circular extended source. The latter fact is not obvious theoretically (has to do with properties of computer graphics) but should be clear empirically.

In the middle we have the projected density κ from the sims. There may be mass outside the region shown. Such mass is accounted for in the arrival-time surface.

The right column shows the averaged enclosed κ within a given radius. The curve crosses 1 at the Einstein radius. Well, the Einstein radius is strictly defined only for circular lenses. But we can take average enclosed κ equals 1 as an extended definition.

The left and right columns can be compared with the results from SpaghettiLens. The former will test whether the image identification was correct. The latter will test how good the mass estimation was.
